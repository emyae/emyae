---

I spend most of my time at the intersection of two things I care about: large speech models, and the phonological detail that makes Pacific languages themselves. My family is from the Big Island — the ʻohana settled in Hilo four generations ago — and I grew up hearing ʻŌlelo Hawaiʻi in the cracks of an otherwise English-speaking childhood. The research followed from the upbringing.

## Research

- **Speech foundation models** — fine-tuning and evaluation for under-represented languages, particularly the Polynesian family (Hawaiian, Māori, Sāmoan, Tongan, Tahitian).
- **Multimodal LLMs** — how jointly-trained audio-text models represent phonological contrasts that bare text models collapse.
- **Culture × computation** — the not-very-fashionable claim that building for communities who speak *your* language changes which benchmarks you should care about.

`Speech foundation models` · `Multimodal LLMs` · `Low-resource ASR` · `Phonology-aware codecs` · `Revitalization-facing NLP`
